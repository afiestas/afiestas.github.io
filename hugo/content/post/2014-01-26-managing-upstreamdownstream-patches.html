In my last post "Demand a KDE Experience" one of the most discussed points was downstream patches, what do we do with them?

You might not know but I have been working really close with the Kubuntu team during the last 2 years, one of my tasks as the upstream was reviewing all the patches they had in Qt/KDE packages. From that experience and from what I remember this is  a list of the most common types of patches you can find applied on our software:

  1. Distribution integration
    1. Patches to install extra software (codecs, flash, etc)
    2. Things related to the Package System
  2. Rejected features upstream
  3. Backports
  4. Workdaroundsruse
